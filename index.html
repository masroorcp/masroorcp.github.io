About me

I am a cosmologist interested in theories of gravity to understand the Dark Sector of the Universe
and Testing Gravity Theories: from cosmology to strong gravity systems, like Black Holes. At present,
I am a postdoc fellow at the Centre for Theoretical Physics and Natural Philosophy at Mahidol University,
Thailand. Before joining this position, I held a short-term postdoc at Yukawa Institute for Theoretical
Physics, Kyoto University, where I finished my thesis work on Theoretical and Observational Constraints
on the Cosmology of Theories of Gravity.

Research
Interests

My research interest primarily falls on theories of gravity. Einstein's theory of gravity, General Relativity (GR), is the mother of modern gravity theory. I have explored theories beyond GR. The natural question is, why do we need theories beyond GR? It is the famous Cosmological Constant (CC) problem. To understand our universe, we impose the cosmological principle with CC and assume the presence of cold dark matter. The resulting model is called LambdaCDM. This model is remarkably successful in explaining the cosmological data. However, there are cracks in this model, which is named Cosmological Tensions. My research falls under these fascinating areas.
